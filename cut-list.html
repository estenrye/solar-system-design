Important Notes — Before Ordering

▸ Rafter length verification: The actual tilt angle of this structure is ~10.3° (4′ rise over 22′ run), NOT 45°. True 45° would require equal rise and run (22′ rise and 22′ run). Verify actual rafter lengths by measuring in field from installed beam heights before ordering all material.

▸ Lumber availability: 22′ lengths of 2×10 may not be available at big-box stores — check local lumber yards. Alternative: use 2× 12′ lengths with a centered splice plate (doubled 2×10 splice plate, 24″ long, bolted). LVL beams at 22′ are standard from lumber yards and recommended.

▸ PT lumber drying: Freshly treated PT lumber contains moisture. Allow to dry 6–8 weeks or use KDAT (kiln-dried after treatment) lumber to prevent warping. Especially important for rafters.

▸ Add 10% waste factor to all lumber quantities. Add 5% to hardware quantities for installation errors and spares.

▸ Wind load check: Minnesota 90 mph design wind speed. Rafters at 24″ OC with 22′ span — engineer should confirm 2×8 is adequate or upgrade to 2×10 rafters. Solar panel wind uplift adds load; IronRidge provides load tables per array.
