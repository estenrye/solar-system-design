General Structural Notes
All lumber to be #1 or better Douglas Fir or Southern Yellow Pine
Posts: 6×6 ACQ pressure treated, rated for ground contact (UC4B)
Footings: 18″ diameter sonotube, min 42″ deep below frost line (MN frost depth ~42″)
Concrete: minimum 3,000 PSI at 28 days, 6-bag mix
Front beams: doubled 2×10 #1 DF, bolted with (2) 1/2″ × 6″ through bolts @ 24″ OC
Rear beams: doubled 2×10 #1 DF, same fastening as front
Rafters: 2×8 #1 DF @ 24″ OC, toenailed to beams (2) 10d nails each end
Post bases: Simpson ABU66Z or equal, galvanized, (4) 1/2″ anchor bolts per base
All hardware: hot-dip galvanized or stainless steel — no plain steel fasteners
IronRidge XR100 rail: L-foot mounts @ every rafter — (1) 3/8″×3″ lag per L-foot
Panel clearance: min 24″ from bottom panel edge to gravel — verify with rafter height
Row gap: 3″ minimum between upper and lower panel rows for snow shed
Lateral bracing: install Simpson Strong-Tie diagonal bracing at corner bays
Wind uplift: verify rafter-to-beam connection for MN 90 mph design wind speed
Snow load: design for 40 psf ground snow load (Minnesota), panels reduce accumulation
Permit: structure requires City of Bloomington building permit — engineer of record to stamp footing design
