Generator Integration Details
How Generator Auto-Start Works
1. Grid fails OR battery SOC drops below set threshold
2. GridBOSS sends 2-wire dry-contact signal → generator starts
3. Programmable warm-up period (60–120 sec typical)
4. Transfer switch engages → gen powers loads + charges batteries
5. When batteries recharged to set level, gen disconnects
6. Programmable cool-down before generator shuts off

100A → 200A Service Upgrade
Why It's Required · What's Involved · Cost · Xcel Coordination
Required for GridBOSS
Required for 12+ kW solar system
Covered by MN sales tax exemption
⚠ MANDATORY The GridBOSS requires a 200A service entrance with the Eaton CSR2200N main breaker. Your current 100A service cannot feed it. This upgrade must be completed before or simultaneously with solar installation. It is the first task in the electrician scope of work.
Why Your 100A Service Must Be Upgraded
1. GridBOSS Hardware Requirement
The GridBOSS is rated as a 200A service entrance device. Its Eaton CSR2200N main breaker is a 200A unit. You cannot simply swap it for a 100A breaker and maintain UL certification or permit compliance. The device is designed to replace your existing main service entrance — it must match your utility feed ampacity.
2. Inverter Load Requirements
Each FlexBOSS21 uses a 90A breaker in the GridBOSS. Two FlexBOSS21 units = 180A of inverter capacity alone. Add home loads, generator port, and smart port circuits — your system fundamentally requires 200A service. A 100A feed would be undersized even for a single-inverter installation of this scale.
3. Good News: GridBOSS IS the Upgrade
The GridBOSS functions as your new service entrance panel — it replaces your current main breaker panel. You're not adding a 200A panel and a GridBOSS; the GridBOSS IS the 200A panel. This means the service upgrade and solar installation are a single coordinated job, not two separate projects.
What the Upgrade Involves
Xcel Energy Coordination
1. Contact Xcel to request 200A service upgrade
2. Xcel inspects existing service drop from pole/underground to meter
3. If service lateral (wire from Xcel to your meter) is undersized for 200A, Xcel upgrades their portion (usually at their cost or low cost)
4. You upgrade meter base and everything on your side of the meter
5. City of Bloomington electrical permit required
6. Xcel re-inspection and reconnect

Electrician Scope of Work — Service Upgrade + Solar Combined
Step 1 — Permits
Pull City of Bloomington electrical permit. File Xcel interconnection application. Notify Xcel of 200A service upgrade request. Typical timeline: 2–4 weeks approval.
Step 2 — Service Entrance
Xcel disconnects and upgrades utility-side lateral if needed. Install new 200A meter base. Mount GridBOSS as new service entrance. Install Eaton CSR2200N 200A main breaker.
Step 3 — Solar Tie-In
Install FlexBOSS21(s) with 90A breakers in GridBOSS hybrid ports. Connect DC from arrays to inverters. Connect battery bank to inverters. Run all conduit and wiring per NEC.
Step 4 — Load Migration
Re-home all existing circuits from old 100A panel to GridBOSS backed-up and non-backed-up outputs. Configure smart ports. Set up generator port wiring. Commission and test. Inspection sign-off.
