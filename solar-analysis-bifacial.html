Albedo Guide — Patio Surface Selection
Your plan uses white limestone gravel — an excellent balance of cost (~$300/array), aesthetics as a functional patio surface, and albedo performance. In Minnesota winters, snow covering the gravel temporarily raises albedo to 0.80–0.90, delivering peak rear-side irradiance precisely when solar production is otherwise at its lowest.

Bifacial-Specific Design Requirements
Min Ground Clearance
20–24 in
NREL recommends ≥20" from bottom panel edge to ground. Your 6ft front posts achieve this easily. Minimum bottom rail height = 24" in final design.
Row Gap Spacing
3–5 in
Gap between stacked portrait rows allows snow to shed between rows and fall to the ground beneath — adding to albedo and preventing pile-up at lower rail.
Racking Rear Clearance
Open Frame
IronRidge XR rails are open-frame by design — rear surface fully exposed. No solid purlins or opaque mounting hardware behind panel rear face. Specify bifacial-compatible clamps only.
Wiring / Shading
Route Down
Run all DC wiring along front face of rafter or down the posts — never zip-tied across the rear of the panels. Rear-face wiring shadows degrade bifacial performance. Use conduit on post faces.
